MY ACADEMIC JOURNEY

As a Graduate Entry Medicine student at Gulf Medical University, I have been dedicated to acquiring knowledge and skills that lay a strong foundation for my medical career.

01 · Foundation

Learning and Growth

My academic foundation at Gulf Medical University is built on rigorous medical science, integrated systems-based learning, and a commitment to evidence-based practice. Each module is approached with structured curiosity and analytical depth.

I continuously engage with primary literature, clinical case discussions, and guided self-study to strengthen my understanding of pathophysiology, pharmacology, and clinical medicine — always connecting theory to real-world application.

02 · Practice

Clinical Skills

Clinical skills training has equipped me with essential patient interaction competencies through simulation and real-world experiences. I have developed proficiency in history-taking, systematic physical examination, and clinical documentation.

Each session in the clinical skills laboratory is followed by structured reflection, allowing me to identify learning gaps and consolidate techniques before transitioning to patient-facing environments. This iterative approach builds both confidence and competence.

03 · Experience

Hands-On Training

Hands-on training in medical procedures provides practical experience under expert supervision. My observership at CKS Hospital, Jaipur allowed me to observe ward rounds, intensive care management, and four surgical procedures across different specialties.

This real-world exposure transformed theoretical knowledge into lived clinical understanding — from reading about tympanic membrane grafting to watching a specialist perform the procedure while explaining each step in real-time.
