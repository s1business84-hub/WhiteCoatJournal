Personal Reflection

A

Description of the Activity

What happened — the factual account

During this semester, I co-founded and began developing Electivio, a digital platform designed to connect medical students across the UAE and wider Middle East with hospitals offering structured clinical observership and elective placements. The initiative arose from a gap I identified in my own medical education: the lack of a centralised, standardised system for students to access clinical electives, leaving the process fragmented and inequitable.

Working with a small team of fellow students, I took a leadership role in shaping the platform's vision, coordinating team members, and initiating outreach to hospitals. This project demanded collaboration across disciplines, creative problem-solving, and the ability to translate real clinical education needs into technical solutions. Alongside this, I continued in my role as a PAL Supervisor, supporting peer tutors and student learners, while also participating in MEDHACK — a medical innovation hackathon that brought together students, engineers, and healthcare professionals to develop solutions to real clinical challenges.

These three parallel activities — PAL mentorship, Electivio platform development, and MEDHACK participation — collectively demonstrate my commitment to collaboration, innovation, and leadership at the student level. Each involved identifying gaps in healthcare education and professional practice, and taking proactive steps within teams to address them.

B

Self-Reflection

Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Step 1 Description

This semester, I co-founded Electivio with a small group of peers to build a platform connecting medical students with hospitals for clinical electives. The project involved leading peers, communicating with external stakeholders, and coordinating across institutions. Simultaneously, I continued serving as a PAL Supervisor at GMU and participated in MEDHACK as part of an interdisciplinary team developing healthcare solutions to real clinical challenges.

Step 2 Feelings

I felt energised by both opportunities. Taking ownership of a project based on a real frustration I experienced as a student was empowering. Although I felt uncertain at times — especially with platform development and hospital outreach — leading a team toward a shared vision gave me a strong sense of purpose beyond traditional coursework. My PAL role added another layer to this: supporting peers' academic growth brought a quiet satisfaction that is distinct from individual academic achievement.

Step 3 Evaluation

Positive aspects included the team's motivation, early traction with the Electivio concept, and formal recognition of my leadership role at MedConf. MEDHACK reinforced the value of rapid, cross-disciplinary collaboration — and how healthcare solutions improve when engineering, clinical, and design perspectives converge. The main challenge was balancing these responsibilities with academic demands, which required deliberate prioritisation and time management. Coordinating with team members who had different schedules also highlighted the need for structured communication frameworks.

Step 4 Analysis

Through the lens of Theme 5 — Collaborator, Innovator & Leader — these three experiences connect strongly to collaboration, innovation, and leadership in healthcare. Medical education research emphasises that innovation in healthcare systems, including education infrastructure, is an important form of health leadership. Electivio addresses a genuine gap that affects the quality of future clinicians' training and, indirectly, patient care. My PAL role reinforces that effective collaboration is not limited to clinical teams — it includes the learning community that shapes who clinicians become. MEDHACK demonstrated that innovation in medicine is most powerful when it is interdisciplinary and responsive to real system problems. These experiences collectively confirm that healthcare leadership extends well beyond clinical settings.

Step 5 Conclusion

This experience taught me that innovation in healthcare is not limited to clinical practice. I strengthened my ability to lead without formal authority, manage team dynamics, and support a shared vision across multiple simultaneous initiatives. I also realised that seeking faculty mentorship earlier would have improved our progress on Electivio and helped align the platform with academic and institutional standards. The diversity of my concurrent roles — PAL supervisor, platform co-founder, and hackathon collaborator — has given me a multi-dimensional understanding of what it means to work effectively within and across teams in healthcare education.

Step 6 Action Plan

Going forward, I will present Electivio to faculty supervisors this semester to seek formal mentorship and feedback, with the goal of establishing at least one hospital partnership by the end of the academic year. In my PAL role, I will introduce more structured debrief sessions for tutors to improve consistency of peer teaching quality and strengthen the PAL supervision framework at GMU. I will continue developing my leadership and collaboration skills through peer advising and interprofessional activities, and I will pursue further innovation challenges or hackathons to sustain and deepen the problem-solving mindset and interdisciplinary collaboration skills developed through MEDHACK.
