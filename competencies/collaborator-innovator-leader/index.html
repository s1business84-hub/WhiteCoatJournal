Activities & Evidence

2024 – Present Peer-Assisted Learning

PAL Supervisor — Gulf Medical University

Appointed as a Peer-Assisted Learning (PAL) Supervisor at GMU, advising student tutors, ensuring teaching commitments are met, and maintaining strong connections with student learners to enhance the academic environment. The role requires interpersonal leadership, accountability, and the ability to mentor without formal authority.

2024 Innovation & Hackathon

MEDHACK — Medical Innovation Hackathon

Participated in MEDHACK, a medical innovation hackathon bringing together medical students, engineers, and healthcare professionals to develop solutions addressing real clinical and healthcare system challenges. The experience developed rapid prototyping skills, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the ability to communicate complex ideas under time constraints.

2024 – Present Founder & Platform Development

Electivio — Clinical Elective Placement Platform

Founded Electivio (electivio.space), a digital platform connecting medical students across the UAE and wider Middle East with hospitals offering structured clinical observership and elective placements. Led the platform's vision, coordinated the founding team, and initiated hospital outreach — addressing a real gap in healthcare education infrastructure that I experienced as a student.

Personal Reflection

A

Description of the Activity

What happened — the factual account

This semester, I founded Electivio — a platform connecting medical students across the UAE and Middle East with hospitals offering clinical elective placements — addressing a gap I identified in my own education. I led the platform's vision, coordinated the team, and initiated hospital outreach. Alongside this, I continued as a PAL Supervisor supporting peer tutors, and participated in MEDHACK, a medical innovation hackathon bringing together students, engineers, and clinicians to solve real healthcare challenges.

B

Self-Reflection

Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Step 1 Description

I founded Electivio, leading peers and coordinating hospital outreach while simultaneously serving as PAL Supervisor at GMU and participating in MEDHACK as part of an interdisciplinary team developing healthcare solutions.

Step 2 Feelings

Taking ownership of a project rooted in a real gap I experienced was empowering, though uncertainty around platform development and hospital outreach was real. My PAL role added another dimension — supporting peers' growth brought satisfaction distinct from individual academic achievement.

Step 3 Evaluation

The team's motivation and early Electivio traction were encouraging. MEDHACK reinforced the value of rapid, cross-disciplinary collaboration. The main challenge was balancing these initiatives with academic demands, requiring deliberate prioritisation and structured communication.

Step 4 Analysis

Electivio addresses a genuine gap in clinical education, affecting the quality of future clinicians' training. My PAL role reinforces that collaboration extends to the learning community that shapes who clinicians become. MEDHACK demonstrated that medical innovation is most powerful when interdisciplinary and responsive to real system problems. Together, these experiences confirm that healthcare leadership extends well beyond clinical settings.

Step 5 Conclusion

I strengthened my ability to lead without formal authority and manage team dynamics across simultaneous initiatives. I also recognised that seeking faculty mentorship earlier would have accelerated Electivio's progress and better aligned it with institutional standards.

Step 6 Action Plan

I will present Electivio to faculty supervisors and target at least one hospital partnership this academic year. In my PAL role, I will introduce structured debrief sessions to strengthen peer teaching quality. I will pursue further innovation challenges to deepen the interdisciplinary problem-solving mindset developed through MEDHACK.
