B

Self-Reflection

Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Step 1 Description

From 20–27 March 2026, I completed an observership at CKS Hospital, Jaipur, under Dr. Prakash Chandwani, a Consultant Cardiologist, spanning ward rounds, ICU, and operating theatre.

Ward Rounds: I checked on all patients with the clinical team, observing how experienced clinicians synthesise clinical information into evidence-based decisions and communicate management plans with families.

ICU: I learned about haemodynamic monitoring, ABG interpretation, ventilator management, and how critically ill patients are rapidly assessed and stabilized. Understanding how vital signs and blood gas values inform clinical decision-making in acute care was both challenging and formative.

Operating Theatre: I observed four surgical procedures with real-time teaching: tympanoade membrane graft, knee replacement, prostate enlargement resection, and vocal cord biopsy. The surgeons explained operative steps and anatomy. Studying prostate enlargement concurrently in lectures allowed me to connect theory to the actual tissue planes and strategies I observed.

Step 2 Feelings

The ward was immediately engaging — textbook content came alive at the bedside. The ICU was humbling: proximity to suffering was confronting, and I observed not just interventions but the team's composure navigating difficult conversations. The theatre generated quiet awe — the precision and choreography of a functioning surgical team.

Step 3 Evaluation

Senior clinicians were welcoming and created rich teaching moments. The breadth of environments (ward, ICU, theatre) was invaluable for understanding care continuity across the hospital system.

Step 4 Analysis

Patient-centred care demands advocacy and clear communication. The operating theatre revealed that technical skill is one layer of operative excellence — situational awareness, team hierarchy, and sterile discipline are equally critical to patient safety.

Step 5 Conclusion

This observership crystallised my motivation for medicine and sharpened my understanding of competent, compassionate patient care. It made me more honest about the distance between where I am now and where I need to be — and that honesty is itself a professional asset.

Step 6 Action Plan

I will seek further observership opportunities and develop structured debriefs. I will study ICU monitoring parameters and surgical anatomy to contextualise my observations.
