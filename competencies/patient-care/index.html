A

Description of the Activity

What happened — the factual account

From 20–27 March 2026, I completed a structured observership at CKS Hospital, Jaipur, under the supervision of Dr. Prakash Chandwani (Consultant Cardiologist). The placement was independently arranged to gain early clinical exposure. During the week, I attended ward rounds, ICU reviews, and operating theatre sessions; observed clinical assessments, investigations, and multidisciplinary management; and gained insight into professional communication, ethical practice, and hospital workflow. The observership was formally recognised with a certificate issued on 27 April 2026.

B

Self-Reflection

Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Step 1 Description

The observership involved exposure to three key settings:

  • Ward rounds: observing patient reviews, clinical reasoning, and communication of management plans.
  • ICU: learning haemodynamic monitoring, ABG interpretation, ventilator use, and rapid stabilization of critically ill patients.
  • Operating theatre: observing four procedures (tympanic membrane graft, knee replacement, prostate resection, vocal cord biopsy), with real-time teaching on anatomy and surgical decision-making. A notable experience was correlating prostate enlargement taught in lectures with intraoperative findings.
Step 2 Feelings

Ward rounds were intellectually engaging, as theoretical knowledge translated into clinical decision-making. The ICU was initially overwhelming due to the severity of illness and emotional intensity, but it highlighted the importance of composure and communication. The operating theatre evoked admiration for precision and teamwork. Overall, the experience was both motivating and humbling.

Step 3 Evaluation

The observership provided valuable, diverse exposure across clinical environments, enhancing understanding of patient care continuity. Clinicians were supportive and actively explained their reasoning. However, the lack of structured feedback limited opportunities for targeted improvement and consolidation of learning.

Step 4 Analysis

This experience demonstrated that clinical reasoning is iterative, evolving with new patient data. ICU exposure emphasized the importance of integrating physiological parameters, such as haemodynamics and ABG values, into urgent decision-making. The operating theatre reinforced that surgical excellence requires not only technical skill but also situational awareness, teamwork, and adherence to sterile protocols. Additionally, effective patient-centred care extends beyond treatment to include clear and empathetic communication with patients and families.

Step 5 Conclusion

This observership was a significant formative experience that bridged theoretical knowledge with clinical practice. It strengthened my motivation for medicine and clarified the competencies required for effective and compassionate patient care. It also highlighted gaps in my clinical understanding that require further development.

Step 6 Action Plan

I plan to pursue additional observerships in internal medicine, paediatrics, and emergency medicine to broaden clinical exposure. I will implement structured self-reflection after each experience and focus on improving my understanding of ICU parameters, ABG interpretation, and surgical anatomy. I will also seek regular feedback to guide my clinical development.
