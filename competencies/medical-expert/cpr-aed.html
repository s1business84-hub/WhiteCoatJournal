Reflection · Medical Expert

CPR & AED Certification

August 2024 · Dubai Health Authority – Hayat Institute

Personal Reflection

A

Description of the Activity

What happened — the factual account

In August 2024, I completed a CPR and AED certification course run by the Dubai Health Authority in collaboration with Hayat Institute. The training combined theoretical instruction with supervised practical sessions covering adult, child, and infant resuscitation protocols, including the safe and correct use of an automated external defibrillator. I enrolled independently to strengthen my emergency-response skills beyond the standard curriculum and to ensure I could act confidently in a real cardiac arrest situation. Upon successful completion, I received the official DHA-accredited certificate pictured above.

Open certificate ↗
B

Self-Reflection

Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Step 1 Description

The course covered chest compressions, rescue breathing, and safe AED operation across adult, child, and infant scenarios. Instructors delivered theory, then moved immediately into hands-on stations where each technique was practised with direct feedback. I completed all modules and passed the certification assessment.

Step 2 Feelings

Initially anxious — particularly around compression depth, rate, and correct AED pad placement under timed conditions. As the session progressed, the structured environment and opportunity to repeat each step replaced that anxiety with growing confidence and a clearer sense of clinical responsibility.

Step 3 Evaluation

The course was well-structured: demonstrations were followed by immediate, specific feedback. Instructors consistently emphasised teamwork, role assignment, and closed-loop communication during simulated arrests. The main limitation was brief group practice time, which restricted how many times I could independently lead a full scenario from recognition through to handover.

Step 4 Analysis

Successful resuscitation depends on far more than technical proficiency — it requires calm leadership, coordinated team roles, and clear closed-loop communication. Understanding the physiological rationale behind adequate compression depth, minimal interruptions, full recoil, and timing of defibrillation deepened my respect for evidence-based emergency protocols. The AED module was particularly instructive: early defibrillation is the single most impactful intervention in ventricular fibrillation, making device confidence a genuine clinical obligation.

Step 5 Conclusion

CPR and AED competence is a fundamental ethical responsibility of every clinician — not an optional supplement. The certification improved both my technical skill and psychological readiness to act decisively. It also reinforced that emergency response is a team endeavour: no single clinician resuscitates a patient; a well-coordinated team does.

Step 6 Action Plan

I will renew my CPR and AED certification before expiry and pursue ACLS training during clinical years. I will actively seek supervised opportunities to apply these skills in hospital settings, and encourage peers to maintain current certification — contributing to a safer learning and clinical environment for everyone.
