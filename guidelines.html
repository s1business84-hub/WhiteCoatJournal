⚠️ Critical Requirements

Mandatory Submission Criteria

⚡
Mark Deduction Warning

Failure to adhere to word count limits (300-500 words per reflection) will result in mark deduction.

Your portfolio submission must include complete evidence and reflection structured using the Gibbs Reflective Cycle with all 6 stages clearly documented.

  • Portfolio Duration
    14-week collection period (entire semester). Collect evidence continuously throughout the semester—do not leave it to the last minute.
  • Minimum Competencies
    Collect evidence for AT LEAST 2 competencies across the semester. Show breadth of learning across multiple domains.
  • Single Submission
    Submit ONLY 1 competency for formal evaluation and grading. Choose your strongest, most comprehensive work.
  • Word Count Enforcement
    Reflection: 300-500 words minimum. Exceeding or falling short results in mark penalties per rubric.
  • Gibbs Reflective Cycle
    All 6 stages must be visibly documented: Description → Feelings → Evaluation → Analysis → Conclusion → Action Plan
  • Supporting Evidence
    Include exam scores, certificates, supervisor comments, concept maps, community participation records, or peer feedback as applicable.

🔄 Reflection Structure

The Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Every reflection must follow the 6-stage Gibbs Reflective Cycle. This structured approach ensures deep, meaningful reflection on your clinical experiences.

1
Description
What happened? Provide objective facts about the experience. Include WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, and CIRCUMSTANCES. What was your role? Who was involved? What was the context?
2
Feelings
What did you feel? Describe your emotional responses. Did you feel confident, anxious, excited, uncertain? What aspects triggered these feelings?
3
Evaluation
What was good or bad? Analyze what went well and what didn't. Did you meet learning objectives? Were outcomes positive? What surprised you?
4
Analysis
Why did it happen? Deep dive into causes and contributing factors. Connect to theory, competencies, and your learning. What did you learn? What skills were demonstrated?
5
Conclusion
What now? Summarize key learning points. Link to competency frameworks. What do you now understand differently about clinical practice?
6
Action Plan
How will you apply this? Identify specific actions to take forward. What will you do differently next time? How will you continue to develop this competency?
✓
All 6 Stages Required

Each stage must be visibly labeled and clearly documented. Use headings, color coding, or visual separators to make the structure obvious to assessors. Incomplete cycles will result in lower marks.

📊 Grading Criteria

Assessment Rubric (Out of 15 points)

Criterion Novice (1) Developing (2) Competent (3) Advanced (4-5)
Organization & Structure (0-5) Lacks clear organization; Gibbs cycle incomplete or unclear Some structure present; 3-4 Gibbs stages visible All 6 Gibbs stages present; well-organized; minor gaps Exceptionally organized; all stages clear with excellent flow; professional presentation
Quality of Evidence (0-5) Minimal or irrelevant evidence; lacks supporting materials Some evidence provided; incomplete documentation Good evidence; includes exam scores, supervisor comments, or certificates Comprehensive, well-documented evidence; multiple evidence types; clear competency links
Quality of Reflection (0-5) Surface-level reflection; no connection to learning; generic observations Basic reflection; some analysis; limited depth Good reflection; connects experience to theory; identifies learning; mostly meets word count Deep, insightful reflection; strong theory connections; clear competency demonstration; 300-500 words exactly
Scoring Scale

0-5 points: Below expectations — Significant gaps, incomplete reflection, minimal evidence

6-10 points: Meets expectations — Adequate structure, decent reflection, supporting evidence present

11-15 points: Exceeds expectations — Excellent organization, insightful reflection, comprehensive evidence, perfect word count compliance
