Overview

Observership at CKS Hospital

During March 2026, I undertook a clinical observership under Dr. Prakash Chandwani, a renowned cardiologist at CKS Hospital, Jaipur. This experience provided direct exposure to patient management, multidisciplinary care, and operative procedures across various departments.

This observership improved my ability to connect classroom knowledge with real clinical practice. It also enhanced my observational skills, understanding of patient-centered care, and appreciation for teamwork within healthcare systems.

Ward Rounds

Daily Ward Rounds & Patient Review

My daily responsibilities involved accompanying the clinical team during ward rounds, where I observed patient evaluations, clinical discussions, and decision-making processes. I engaged in reviewing patient histories, monitoring progress, and understanding treatment plans.

This allowed me to appreciate the importance of structured clinical reasoning and continuity of care. Observing how the senior physicians moved fluidly between clinical assessment, patient communication, and treatment adjustment deepened my respect for the cognitive complexity of medicine.

"Continuity of care is not a schedule — it is a relationship between a clinician's understanding and a patient's evolving condition."

ICU Exposure

Intensive Care Unit & Critical Monitoring

I also spent time in the intensive care unit, where I was introduced to critical care monitoring, including interpretation of arterial blood gases and patient stability parameters. This exposure enhanced my understanding of acute patient management and the importance of timely intervention.

The ICU environment was transformative — the pace, precision, and collaborative urgency of critical care challenged me to think more systematically about physiological parameters and the sequencing of clinical interventions. I observed how small deviations in ABG values translated into immediate changes in management plans.

ABG Interpretation Critical Care Monitoring Acute Management Haemodynamic Assessment
Surgical Exposure

Surgical Procedures Observed

Additionally, I observed multiple surgical procedures including tympanic membrane grafting, knee replacement surgery, prostate enlargement surgery, and vocal cord biopsy. During these procedures, the surgeons provided step-by-step explanations of the operative techniques, anatomical structures, and clinical rationale behind each intervention.

A particularly impactful experience was observing prostate surgery while simultaneously studying the same topic academically. This integration of theoretical learning with real surgical exposure significantly strengthened my understanding and retention of the subject — the most important insight of the entire observership.

CPR & AED — Reflective Analysis

A three-part reflective account of how the Dubai Heart Safe City CPR & AED certification shaped my development as a Medical Expert.

01

From Uncertainty to Confidence

Initially, I felt cautious about my ability to perform resuscitative procedures with precision, especially under pressure. However, repeated supervised practice, detailed feedback, and guided correction helped transform this uncertainty into confidence. This growth highlighted a key attribute of the Medical Expert role: the ability to evolve through reflective practice, continuous learning, and deliberate skill refinement.

I learned to assess scenarios rapidly, identify priorities, and intervene effectively using standardised CPR protocols. The certification also demonstrated that technical proficiency alone cannot ensure successful resuscitation. Emergency response demands coordinated teamwork, situational awareness, clear communication, and decisive action. Understanding how to integrate these components reinforced my appreciation of the Medical Expert's responsibility to collaborate seamlessly with healthcare providers while maintaining clinical leadership during emergencies.

02

Evidence Behind the Protocol

Learning the evidence behind CPR guidelines deepened my respect for research-driven practice. Understanding why each action matters physiologically and statistically improved my adherence to best practices and enhanced my ability to make informed decisions in time-sensitive situations. This experience underscored the importance of maintaining updated clinical knowledge and continually aligning my actions with current evidence-based protocols.

Reflecting on this certification reinforced the ethical dimension of the Medical Expert role. As someone entrusted with patient care, I recognised the obligation to remain competent, proactive, and prepared for emergencies, regardless of the setting. This realization motivated me to commit to regular recertification, additional life-support training, and ongoing clinical exposure to strengthen my readiness for future responsibilities.

03

Shaping My Identity as a Medical Expert

Overall, the CPR/AED course significantly enhanced my procedural skills, emergency communication, and clinical judgment. It has shaped my identity as a developing Medical Expert, ensuring I can respond effectively, confidently, and ethically in situations where immediate intervention may determine survival.

This certification now serves as a cornerstone of my clinical portfolio — not merely as a credential, but as a lived experience that transformed my understanding of what it means to be prepared, responsible, and competent in the face of medical emergencies. Every future clinical encounter will be approached with the same rigour and commitment that this training instilled in me.
