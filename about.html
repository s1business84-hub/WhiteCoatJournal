My Story

Building a Foundation
for Exceptional Care

I am an MBBS student at Gulf Medical University, driven by a deep commitment to understanding medicine beyond textbooks. My journey combines clinical exposure, academic excellence, leadership, and entrepreneurship to build a strong foundation for future patient care.

Through structured observerships, academic training, continuous self-evaluation, and leadership roles, I aim to develop competencies across clinical reasoning, communication, professionalism, and evidence-based medicine.

As the Founder of Electivio.space, I created a platform connecting medical students with clinical elective opportunities worldwide. As a PAL (Peer-Assisted Learning) Supervisor, I advise tutors, ensure they complete their teaching commitments, and maintain strong connections with students to enhance the learning environment.

As MedHack University Lead, I spread awareness about healthcare innovation, organize hackathons, and foster a culture of creative problem-solving in medicine. I also organized and participated in GMU Alumni Events focused on peer learning and professional development.

I believe in improving myself by 1% every day—physically, mentally, and emotionally. My long-term goal is to build a career that not only heals but also empowers, educates, and inspires others through evidence-based care and continuous learning.

"Every clinical encounter is an opportunity to learn — not only about disease, but about people, empathy, and the profound responsibility of medicine. True impact comes from continuous growth and giving back to the community."
