About Me

Building a Legacy Through
Innovation & Patient Impact

I am a medical student at Gulf Medical University (GMU), Ajman, UAE, currently completing a Degree in Biomedical Science with a passion for clinical excellence, healthcare innovation, and patient-centered care. Graduating in 2029, my journey has been shaped by early clinical exposure, structured reflection, and a commitment to building competency across all dimensions of medical practice.

My clinical interests center on neurosurgery and acute care medicine, driven by fascination with the nervous system and the ability to make rapid, life-saving decisions. Beyond the clinic, I am an entrepreneur and healthcare innovator—I founded Electivio, a digital platform connecting medical students across the Middle East with structured clinical electives, addressing a real gap in healthcare education infrastructure.

I serve as a PAL Supervisor at GMU, mentoring peer tutors and supporting student learning in a leadership role without formal authority. I have completed clinical observerships at CKS Hospital, Jaipur (Cardiology), participated in medical innovation challenges (MEDHACK), and hold certifications in CPR/AED and basic procedural skills including peripheral IV cannulation.

This portfolio documents my reflective practice across nine core medical competencies—evidence of not just what I know, but who I am becoming as a clinician, leader, and innovator. I believe medicine transcends technical skill; it is about understanding the human element of care.
